Scouting Report

OFFENSIVE IDENTITY

Profile
High-power lineup with legitimate threats 1–7. Six hitters with 3+ extra-base hits; team has slugged for power in all game types including district play.
Top Gun
Karson Schulz (#8) is the team's best all-around weapon — .425 AVG, .644 SLG, 7 doubles and 3 HR from the SS/P spot. Low K rate (5 K in 73 AB) shows elite contact ability.
Power Core
Chehresa and Floyd each have 5 HR — strong middle-of-order pop. Wilson slugs .634 in limited AB (6 HR-level extra-base hits in 11 hits). Lindroos adds a triple and consistency.
Tags
Elite AVG Power Heavy Low K — Schulz Multi-HR Threats

PITCHING IDENTITY

Rotation
Antley (2.15 ERA) and McDermott (2.96 ERA / 1.12 WHIP) anchor a capable 1–2. Schulz doubles as a reliable two-way arm (2.43 ERA, 23.0 IP).
Strikeout Arm
McDermott leads with 32 K in 26.0 IP — best K rate on staff. Antley keeps hitters off-balance with movement; only 8 ER allowed in 26 innings.
Concerns
Cupitt (8.88 ERA, 17.1 IP) is a risk factor if rotation is pushed. Durandetto (4.52 ERA, 17 BB in 21.2 IP) shows command issues. Depth is thin past the top 3.
Tags
Antley Ace McDermott K-Rate Cupitt High ERA Thin Depth

STRENGTHS

Lineup Depth
Six hitters above .284 AVG. Schulz–Faudoa–Floyd–Chehresa is one of the best top-four sequences in the district.
Power
Team has accumulated 23 HR across 23 games — nearly 1 HR per game. Elite for HS 6A play.
District Record
4–2 in district, including a dominant 17–1 win over Clear Creek and back-to-back wins over Clear Falls to close out April 2.

WEAKNESSES / WATCH

K Risk
Hunter Floyd leads the team with 24 K in 60 AB (40% K rate). Jackson Wilson has 0 BB in 41 AB — no walk ability creates gaps in the lineup.
Bullpen
Two losses tied to Cupitt/Durandetto implosions. When the top-3 aren't available, the staff ERA climbs sharply.
Bottom Third
Syfert (.235) and Wells (.186) must improve to keep pressure off the power hitters. Wells walks well (11 BB) but doesn't hit enough to be a threat.
